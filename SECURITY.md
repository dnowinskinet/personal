# Security policy

## Scope

This policy covers https://dnowinski.com and this repository's original application code and deployment configuration.

## Out of scope

- GitHub, Cloudflare, email providers, social networks, linked third-party sites, and other infrastructure not controlled by Daniel Nowinski.
- Purely theoretical findings without a plausible impact.
- Dependency-version reports already generated automatically by GitHub, unless an additional exploitable condition is identified.
- Social engineering, phishing, spam, denial-of-service, physical attacks, and attacks against other users.

## Reporting a vulnerability

Send reports to [security@dnowinski.com](mailto:security@dnowinski.com). Include the affected URL or component, a clear description, reproduction steps, potential impact, and relevant screenshots, logs, or proof-of-concept details without unnecessary personal information.

## Research expectations

Act in good faith and minimize access and disruption. Do not access, alter, retain, or disclose another person's information. Stop testing and report promptly if sensitive information is encountered. Do not publicly disclose a vulnerability before a reasonable opportunity to investigate and address it.

Daniel does not intend to pursue action against research conducted in good faith and consistent with this policy. This policy does not authorize violation of law or testing against third-party systems.

## Response

Reports are reviewed on a reasonable best-effort basis. No response or remediation deadline is guaranteed. No bug bounty, payment, reward, or employment offer is promised.
